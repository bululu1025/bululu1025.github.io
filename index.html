Pharmacological Modulation of Fear & Attention

This project investigated how Losartan and Vasopressin modulate human fear response and attention through eye-tracking. We collected multimodal data from 117 participants under pharmacological intervention.

Key Findings:

  • Losartan enhances post-error adaptive control through trial-based learning
  • AVP phase-specifically modulates pupillary-saccade coordination during threat processing
  • Novel computational models successfully differentiated drug effects on threat-response phenotypes

Neural Mechanisms of Looming Threat

fMRI study investigating sex-specific neural dynamics of threat processing in females. Current progress: 14/40 participants acquired.

Methodology:

Using fMRI to study gender-specific threat processing neural dynamics. The study focuses on how males and females differentially process looming threats at the neural level.

Computational Modeling of Threat Response

Developing novel computational models to identify threat-response phenotypes and differentiate drug effects.

Approach:

We developed an FPCA-Cluster-HMM model to identify threat-response phenotypes and differentiate drug effects. This approach allows us to capture the dynamic nature of threat processing and how it's modulated by pharmacological interventions.
